Outcome 1 Questions

1.1

Business today relies almost entirely on IT systems to run. The data a business uses to run is probably stored on a computer system, with backups stored over internet connections elsewhere and on backup hard-drives. If the business has a website, it was be hosted on a server and managed by an IT team. The way the business communicates with customers and other businesses will probably be over the internet. IT systems are the central nervous system of modern businesses and are required for the smooth operation of every section of a business.


1.2

Any project, regardless of the content and scope, requires proper planning first and foremost. Tools like Trello can be used to properly and efficiently keep things in order. If you are doing programming, languages like Python, C++ and all its iterations, HTML, Javascript, Ruby, etc… can be used, all suited for different platforms for the finished projects. If doing penetration testing, tools that exist on a platform like Kali Linux are used to help test the security of a system. And the most important skill, beyond just knowing how to use the tools, is knowing how to transfer knowledge of one program to another, and research how to do what you know is possible in one language to another.

To make this website, first thing is to decide the content. Like these questions that I’m answering at the minute. Next, would be to create a Trello to plan and carry out tasks and keep myself organised in terms of the scope and progression of the site. https://trello.com/b/V0Lygyvg/coding-website-stuart From there, I will research and implement HTML procedures in order to create tables and format the website for insertion of the information I am collating now.


1.4

Digital projects have a unique fault that non-internet based ones don’t, and that is corruption or loss of data from things like power failure of malicious cyber attacks. Keeping backups regularly and making sure to save at regular intervals will keep any loss from being catastrophic. Proper planning is as useful and necessary in IT projects as they are for offline projects as well. Failure can come from a lack of foresight into what is achievable and working to those goals, or the hand of god smacking down your connection or power supply and losing you all that you’ve done. So just plan properly and be prepared so it won’t happen.

1.5

In order to pick the right tool for the job, first you need to know the scale and scope of the project. Are you programming? What is the end-goal for your program? Will it be web-hosted? Back end? Mobile Application? Javascript, HTML, Python and Swift are all used in these, but which is best depends on what you are trying to make with it. Having knowledge of one system is essential, though, and having the knowledge to transfer your knowledge and research how to use that in a different language is most critical.

1.6

Python has helped in the conveniences it’s added to modern versions, highlighting errors in my code and colour coding the rest for ease of understanding. Trello keeps me organised and on task, as well as help give a visual representation of what I still need to do. And GitHub is a useful way of collating all the code written so far together in one easily logged and managed area for use by all people within a team.

1.7

When working to ensure your projects are GDPR compliant, be sure to consider what information you are gathering with this project and how you store it. Keep backups regular and secure, and be sure that your systems are not publically open or have allowed access to more people than is strictly necessary in order to run. The most vulnerable part of any IT system is always the people, so be sure all involved are properly trained in current legal practices for IT and follow them correctly.

Honestly, in a solo setting it feels like just an extra thing I have to go through and make sure is done properly rather than actually helping me. But others, or even in group settings, it is a great tool to make sure everyone knows what needs doing still and organising the project in a clear manner.
